# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Online fitness coaches running their business largely through social content (Instagram first, plus TikTok/YouTube). They're typically past the beginner stage — doing real revenue but capped by the time and discomfort of chasing leads. Their context: posting consistently, fielding DMs, and dreading the "salesy" close. They want more booked discovery calls without turning into a pushy DM-er or running a sleazy funnel. The visitor lands from a post or referral, skeptical of yet another "agency," and is deciding in under a minute whether this feels like them.

## Product Purpose

Atom is a "quiet client engine" for online coaches: content pulls warm leads in, a system filters and books them, and a tailored conversation closes them — no pushy DMs, scripts, or spam. The site's job is to convert a skeptical coach into a booked 20-minute discovery call. Success = the visitor trusts the calm, anti-hard-sell positioning enough to book, backed by proof (testimonials, numbers) and a risk-reversal promise ("5+ booked calls in 30 days or your next quarter is free").

## Brand Personality

Calm and premium. Quiet confidence over hype — restraint is the proof of expertise. Voice is plain-spoken, warm, and a little contrarian: it sells by *refusing* to sell hard ("Made for coaches who hate the hard sell"). Three words: **quiet, confident, deliberate.** It should feel like a trusted operator who's seen it work, not a marketer performing enthusiasm.

## Anti-references

- **Generic SaaS landing template** (the explicit one): gradient hero, identical icon-card grids, big-number metric blocks, tiny tracked uppercase eyebrows above every section, numbered `01 / 02 / 03` scaffolding. The AI-landing-page cliché.
- **Sleazy info-marketer funnels**: fake scarcity, countdown timers, "CRUSH IT" hype, guru neon-on-black — the exact opposite of the brand's anti-hard-sell promise.
- Cold corporate fintech (navy-and-gold, stock handshakes) and cluttered gamified fitness-app UI are also off-brand.

## Design Principles

1. **Sell by not selling.** Every section should lower pressure, not raise it. Calm confidence converts this audience; hype repels it.
2. **Restraint signals expertise.** Whitespace, quiet motion, and a tight palette do the persuading. When in doubt, remove.
3. **Show the proof, don't shout it.** Real numbers, real testimonials, a concrete risk-reversal promise — stated plainly, not dramatized.
4. **One deliberate spectacle.** The animated WebGL light trails are the single brand centerpiece; everything else stays understated so they land. Don't add competing visual noise.
5. **Readability is non-negotiable.** Atmospheric background never wins over legible text — text always sits on a protected, high-contrast surface.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target **WCAG 2.1 AA**: body text ≥4.5:1 contrast (large text ≥3:1), visible keyboard focus, semantic landmarks. The animated background must respect `prefers-reduced-motion` with a static fallback frame, and must never compromise text contrast — the hero scrim and frosted card surfaces exist specifically to keep small text legible over the moving trails.
